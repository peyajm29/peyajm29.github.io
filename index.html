Peya Mowar

I am a research fellow at Microsoft Research India (MSRI), where I work in Technology and Empowerment with Dr. Saikat Guha and Tanuja Ganu.

At MSRI, I build multi-lingual document intelligence systems for making long-form documents, such as books and magazines, accessible to people with print impairment. For the past eight months, I have used instance segmentation and OCR to convert inaccessible newspaper PDFs into accessible HTMLs, which I have delivered daily to 32 users located throughout India.

Previously, I have been fortunate to work with Prof. Khurshid Ahmad at Trinity College Dublin. I graduated with distinct honors from Delhi Technological University, India where my bachelor's thesis was advised by Prof. Dinesh Kumar Vishwakarma.

My research interest lies at the intersection of Responsible Artificial Intelligence and Human-Computer Interaction.

I am applying to PhD programs for Fall'23!

Research

Over the past few years, my interests have evolved from being technology-centered to human-centered. My goal is to address complex societal issues affecting underserved communities through research that spans from needs assessments to deep technical implementation. Representative work is highlighted.

Understanding news consumption behavior of screenreader users
Peya Mowar, Mohit Jain
Working paper, 2022

We conduct interviews and observations to understand the coping mechanisms employed by screenreader users while navigating news from online sources.

Understanding Voice Search Use by Emergent Smartphone Users in India
Meghna Gupta, Peya Mowar, Indrani Medhi Thies
Working paper, 2022

We conduct task-based observations to understand voice search usage among emergent low-literate smartphone users in India.

Towards Optimising OCR for Accessibility
Peya Mowar, Tanuja Ganu, Saikat Guha
AVA: Accessibility, Vision, and Autonomy Meet (CVPR Workshop), 2022
paper

We highlight the importance of preserving visual cues for enhancing the accessibility of long-form content for people with print impairment.

Impact of Sentiments in National Languages: A Hindi Case Study
Peya Mowar, Khurshid Ahmad
In submission, 2021

We evaluate the impact of the sentiments expressed in daily political news published in the Hindi language on the Indian financial markets.

Clickbait in YouTube: Prevention, Detection and Analysis of the Bait
Peya Mowar, Mini Jain, Ruchika Goel, Dinesh Kumar Vishwakarma
Bachelor's Thesis (currently under review), 2021
preprint / dataset

We redefine clickbaits on YouTube by focusing on fake Bollywood videos that have a significant dissonance between their descriptors and content.

Fishing out the Phishing Websites
Peya Mowar, Mini Jain
IEEE International Conference on Cyber Situational Awareness, Data Analytics and Assessment (CyberSA), 2021
paper / dataset

We propose a novel classifier for detecting domain-generated, algorithm-driven phishing websites.

Clickbait in Social Media: Detection and Analysis of the Bait
Mini Jain, Peya Mowar, Ruchika Goel, Dinesh Kumar Vishwakarma
IEEE 55th Annual Conference on Information Sciences and Systems (CISS), 2021
paper

We classify posts on visual-centric social media platforms as clickbaits without the use of user engagement metrics (such as likes, comments).

Emo-GDB Dataset
Peya Mowar, Mini Jain, Ashima Yadav, Dinesh Kumar Vishwakarma
dataset

We develop an affective video dataset with 120k+ keyframes annotated by the emotion elicited by the viewer on watching it.
